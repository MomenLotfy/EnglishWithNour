The Past Continuous Tense

Form of the Past Continuous

The past continuous is formed with was/were + the -ing form of the main verb.

I was watching TV when you called.
They were discussing the project all morning.
She wasn't listening during the meeting.

Actions in Progress

We use the past continuous to describe actions that were ongoing at a specific moment in the past.

"What were you doing at 8 PM last night?" "I was cooking dinner."
When the phone rang, he was taking a shower.

Time Expressions with Past Continuous

Specific moments can be indicated by time expressions or by another past action.

At midnight, they were still driving home.
All afternoon, the children were playing in the garden.
She was reading when the power went out.

Setting the Scene

The past continuous is often used to describe the background situation at the beginning of a story.

The sun was setting, and birds were singing peacefully. Suddenly, a loud noise broke the silence.
People were rushing through the station while announcements were echoing in the hall.

Past Continuous vs. Past Simple

The past continuous describes ongoing actions, while the past simple describes completed actions.

We were watching a movie at 9 PM. (ongoing)
We watched a movie yesterday. (completed)

Longer vs. Shorter Actions

The past continuous describes longer background actions, while the past simple describes shorter interrupting events.

While I was cooking dinner, the phone rang.
They were walking in the park when they met their old friend.

Interrupted Actions

We often use the past continuous for the longer action that gets interrupted by a shorter action in the past simple.

He was running for the bus when he tripped and fell.
While we were having dinner, someone knocked on the door.

Simultaneous Actions

We use the past continuous to describe two or more actions happening at the same time in the past.

While I was studying, my roommate was practicing the guitar.
The chef was cooking while the assistants were preparing the ingredients.

Subtle Differences in Meaning

The sequence of actions can change the meaning of a sentence significantly.

When he arrived, she was making coffee. (She started before he arrived)
When he arrived, she made coffee. (She started after he arrived)
